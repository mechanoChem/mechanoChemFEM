2  * @page Allen_Cahn Example 3 : Allen-Cahn equation
3  * \section Introduction
4  * The Allen Cahn equation is:
5  * \f[
6  \frac{\partial C}{\partial t}=-M\mu
7 \f]
8 *\f[
9 \mu=\frac{\partial g}{\partial C}-\nabla\cdot k\nabla C
10 \f]
11 where \f$g\f$ is a non-convex, ``homogeneous'' free energy density function, whose form has been chosen
12 \f[
13 g(C)=\omega(C-C_\alpha)^2(C-C_\beta)^2
14 \f]
15 The boundary condition is
16 \f[
17 \nabla \mu\cdot\boldsymbol{n}=0 \text{ on }\Gamma
18 \f]
19 The double-well non-convex free energy density function, \f$g(C)\f$, drives segregation of the system into two distinct types.
20 
21 Before implementing the code, we rewrite the equation in the form of standard diffusion-reaction equations:
22 \f[
23 \frac{\partial C}{\partial t}+\nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{j}=r
24 \f]
25 *\f[
26 \boldsymbol{j}=-Mk\nabla C
27 \f]
28 *\f[
29 r=-M\frac{\partial g}{\partial C}=-2M\omega(C-C_\alpha)(C-C_\beta)(2C-C_\alpha-C_\beta)
30 \f]
31 
32 *\section imple Implementation: level 1 user
33 We first define the single scalar primary variable:
34 *\code{.cpp}
35  std::vector<std::vector<std::string> > primary_variables(1);
36  primary_variables[0].push_back("C"); primary_variables[0].push_back("component_is_scalar");
37 \endcode
38 and setup the order of basis function for it:
39 *\code{.cpp}
40 int number_domain=1;
41 int diff_degree=1;
42 std::vector<std::vector<int> > FE_support(number_domain);// store order of polynomial basis functions, 0 means FE_Nothing
43 FE_support[0].push_back(diff_degree);
44 \endcode

74

86 
87 Before we call these two functions, we need the flux and reactions term, we need to first evaluate the values of the primary field and the spatial gradient.
88 We also need to evaluate the value of primary field at previous time step for the Backward Euler time scheme:
89  *\code{.cpp}
90  dealii::Table<1,double> C_conv(n_q_points);
91  dealii::Table<1,Sacado::Fad::DFad<double> > C(n_q_points);
92  dealii::Table<2,Sacado::Fad::DFad<double> > C_grad(n_q_points, dim);
93 
94  evaluateScalarFunction<double,dim>(fe_values, 0, ULocalConv, C_conv);
95  evaluateScalarFunction<Sacado::Fad::DFad<double>,dim>(fe_values, 0, ULocal, C);
96  evaluateScalarFunctionGradient<Sacado::Fad::DFad<double>,dim>(fe_values, 0, ULocal, C_grad);
97 
98  //evaluate diffusion and reaction term
99  dealii::Table<1,Sacado::Fad::DFad<double> > rhs_C(n_q_points);
100  dealii::Table<2,Sacado::Fad::DFad<double> > j_C(n_q_points, dim);
101 
102  j_C=table_scaling<Sacado::Fad::DFad<double>, dim>(C_grad,-kappa*M);
103 
104  for(unsigned int q=0; q<n_q_points;q++){
105  rhs_C[q]=-M*(2*omega*(C[q]-c_alpha)*(C[q]-c_beta)*(2*C[q]-c_alpha-c_beta));
106  }
107 \endcode
