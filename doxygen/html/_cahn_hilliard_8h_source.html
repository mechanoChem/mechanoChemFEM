2  * @page CahnHilliard Example 2 : CahnHilliard with single species
3  * \section Introduction
4  * The Cahn-Hilliard equation is:
5  * \f[
6  \frac{\partial C}{\partial t}=\nabla\cdot(M\nabla\mu)
7 \f]
8 *\f[
9 \mu=\frac{\partial g}{\partial C}-\nabla\cdot k\nabla C
10 \f]
11 where \f$g\f$ is a non-convex, ``homogeneous'' free energy density function, whose form has been chosen
12 \f[
13 g(C)=\omega(C-C_\alpha)^2(C-C_\beta)^2
14 \f]
15 The boundary condiiton is
16 * \f[
17 \nabla\mu\cdot\boldsymbol{n}=0;\quad \nabla C\cdot\boldsymbol{n}=0 \text{ on }\Gamma
18 \f]
19 The double-well non-convex free energy density function, \f$g(C)\f$, drives segregation of the system into two distinct types.
20 
21 Attention is called to the well-known fourth-order nature of this partial differential equation in the concentration \f$C\f$. The polynomial basis can only achieve
22 C0 continuity across the element. To overcome this difficuity, we split the equation into two equations:
23  * \f[
24  \frac{\partial C}{\partial t}+\nabla\cdot(-M\nabla\mu)=0
25 \f]
26 *\f[
27 k\nabla^2 C=\frac{\partial g}{\partial C}-\mu
28 \f]
29 The first equation is diffusion equation, and the second one the Possion equation.
30 *\section imple Implementation: level 1 user
